# Code of Conduct

## The short version

Be decent to people. This project is open to everyone, and everyone deserves to participate without being harassed or made to feel unwelcome.

---

## Our commitment

We're committed to keeping this a harassment-free space for everyone — regardless of age, background, disability, ethnicity, gender identity or expression, level of experience, nationality, appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

---

## What this looks like in practice

**We encourage:**

- Welcoming and constructive feedback
- Disagreeing on technical points while staying respectful
- Acknowledging mistakes, learning, and moving on
- Keeping in mind that everyone here is learning something

**We don't tolerate:**

- Harassment, insults, or personal attacks
- Discriminatory jokes or language
- Publishing someone's private information without permission
- Sustained disruptive behavior in discussions, PRs, or issues
- Any conduct that would reasonably make someone feel unsafe or unwelcome

---

## Scope

This applies everywhere related to the project — GitHub issues, pull requests, discussions, and any other space where people interact on behalf of this project.

---

## Reporting

If you experience or witness behavior that violates this code of conduct, please reach out directly by emailing the maintainer. Reports will be handled with discretion.

All complaints will be reviewed. The maintainer is committed to respecting the privacy of the person reporting an incident.

---

## Enforcement

When a violation is confirmed, the maintainer may take whatever action is appropriate — from a private warning up to a permanent ban from the project, depending on the severity.
